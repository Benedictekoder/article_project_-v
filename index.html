The Amanita Family of Mushrooms

Amanitaceae

Facts

The Amanita family consists of over 600 species. Some are deadly poisonous if eaten, and others are edible. Amanitas can be found across every continent except for the north and southpole. Common caracteristics include white flesh, white gills under the cap, a "skirt" around its stipe (stem) and a bulbenous sack (volva) from which its fruiting body arises.

Amanita Muscaria

With its destinct red colouration and white dots, this is perhaps the most famous member of the Amanita family. Known as the toadstool from fairytales and the mushroom that makes Super Mario enlarged. Amanita Muscaria is poisonous and should never be eaten raw. Comes in several subspecies.

Amanita Rubescens

This Amanita is edible with the correct preparation. It has been described as tasty and delicious. It gets its name from the fact that its white flesh turns redish when bruised or damaged, almost as if its bleeding. The Blusher as it`s also nicknamed, is a common mushroom of the Amanita family.

Amanita virosa

Also called "the Destroying Angel". As its name indicate, this Amanita is deadly poisonous. In its young state, it has been mistaken for other edible species of mushroom which is why it is important to know the identifying markers of the mushroom one forages for. Amanita Virosa is an all white member of the family.
